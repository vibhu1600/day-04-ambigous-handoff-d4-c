# Day 4 Student Challenge Brief

## Day 4 - The Ambiguous Handoff

You are the Execution Communication Owner for HireSignal.

The recruiter pilot is close. The founder wants a quick change:

> Recruiters need to override AI scores and manually shortlist candidates before the pilot. Add it quickly.

This is not a coding task.

This is the moment before bad execution starts.

## Your Mission

Decide what can safely move forward.

You must:

1. read your assigned ambiguity packet
2. identify what is unclear
3. submit one clarification question
4. use the founder response
5. define bounded assumptions
6. escalate risk
7. define minimal safe v1 scope
8. reject unsafe or overbuilt scope
9. produce a founder-ready update
10. defend your decision in viva

## You Get One Clarification Question

Ask one question only.

Make it count.

Weak question:

> Can you explain the feature?

Strong question:

> Should manual override be allowed for any recruiter, or only after a required reason is captured for audit?

## Final Decision

Choose one:

- Proceed
- Proceed conditionally
- Block pending clarification

## What Good Work Looks Like

Strong work:

- names the ambiguity before proposing a solution
- separates known facts from assumptions
- asks a specific clarification question
- defines what is safe for v1
- clearly states what must not be built yet
- challenges the founder's rush and AI's overconfidence
- communicates risk without sounding dramatic

Weak work:

- starts designing screens immediately
- builds everything the founder asked for
- accepts AI's recommendation without context
- hides uncertainty
- gives vague "we need clarity" language
- refuses all work without proposing a safe next step

## Submission

Submit:

1. Ambiguity Resolution Brief
2. 3-minute Stakeholder Update Video
3. Live founder viva
