# CareerForge Day 4 - The Ambiguous Handoff

Product: HireSignal - Recruiter Override Request

Role: Execution Communication Owner

Core lesson:

> If you do not clarify uncertainty, you may confidently build the wrong thing.

## Why Day 4 Exists

Day 1 taught you to debug broken systems.

Day 2 taught you to reason about cascading failures.

Day 3 taught you to verify readiness before trust is lost.

Day 4 moves earlier in the execution chain:

> Can you reduce ambiguity before the wrong work begins?

## Scenario

The recruiter pilot is approaching.

The founder sends:

> Recruiters need to override AI scores and manually shortlist candidates before the pilot. Add it quickly.

That request sounds simple. It is not.

There are unclear permissions, fairness risks, audit concerns, recruiter pressure, and a tempting AI-generated overbuild.

Your job is not to start coding. Your job is to decide what can safely move forward.

## Student Role

You are the Execution Communication Owner.

You are responsible for:

- detecting ambiguity
- asking one high-leverage clarification question
- making bounded assumptions
- escalating risk
- defining minimal safe v1 scope
- rejecting unsafe or overbuilt scope
- sending a founder-ready execution update

## Provided To Solve The Case

- Student challenge brief
- HireSignal scenario package
- Assigned D4-C ambiguity packet
- Ambiguity Resolution Brief template
- Stakeholder Update Video guide

## Outcome Artifacts You Get

- Ambiguity Resolution Brief
- Clarification question
- Bounded assumptions
- Stakeholder Decision Update
- Safe execution decision

## Decision Options

Your final decision must be one of:

- Proceed
- Proceed conditionally
- Block pending clarification

## Solo Challenge

This is a solo challenge.

There is no pair dependency and no student coordination dependency. The founder controls clarification responses.

## Submission Pack

Submit only:

1. Ambiguity Resolution Brief
2. 3-minute Stakeholder Update Video
3. Founder viva

No pair worksheet. No reflection. No separate AI audit. No long API spec.

## AI Policy

AI is allowed. Blind AI is not.

AI may help you organize thoughts or identify possible ambiguity. AI may not decide what is safe to build.

You must challenge both founder assumptions and AI assumptions with context.

## Hard Fail

This fails:

> I would just build the override feature.

So does this:

> AI suggested an admin workflow, so I used that.

Day 4 is about professional execution restraint.
